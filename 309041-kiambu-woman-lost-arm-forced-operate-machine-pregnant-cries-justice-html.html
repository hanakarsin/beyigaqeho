    Kiambu woman who lost arm after being forced to operate machine while pregnant cries for justice

    - Caroline Wangechi was employed at a Chinese-owned company as a cook and cleaner for one year - One of her bosses instructed her to shift from kitchen to machine operation despite being five-month pregnant

    09 Apr 2024, 00:00

    - Caroline Wangechi was employed at a Chinese-owned company as a cook and cleaner for one year

    - One of her bosses instructed her to shift from kitchen to machine operation despite being five-month pregnant

    - The mother of two had no skills and knowledge to operate the recycling machine

    - The Kiambu woman felt dizzy one day and the machine she was operating accidentally crashed her left hand

    When Caroline Wangechi was employed as a cook in a Chinese factory making non-woven fabric one year ago, she served diligently and was lauded by colleagues for her unmatched culinary skills.

    Things went on well until one day, one of her bosses instructed her to shift from the kitchen and begin working as a recycling machine operator.

    At the time of the new assignment, Wambui was five months pregnant and had no skills of operating such a machine in the Juja-based factory.

    Tough economic times and desire to have resources to cater for the angel in her womb, she wore a brave face and took up the assignment after her pleas not to be shifted from cooking role hit a dead end.

    Interestingly, her husband Felix Ochieng' also worked at the same firm, Ecogreen Company's machine section where she had been requested to work.

    Her determination met a life threatening hurdle on Sunday, February 10 2019, a busy day at the machine operation section.

    "I stepped into the machine section which was busy and started working. Shortly after, I began feeling dizzy. I cannot remember exactly what happened later but I felt shattering pain," she reminisced with a traumatised face.

    The middle-aged woman could not comprehend in detail what happened but the lower part of her left hand was severely cut by the machine she was operating.

    "I don't not know what happened. I was feeling dizzy due to the back and forth standing and sitting down. The cloth material I was holding caught my hand inside the machine," she said in an exclusive interview with TUKO.co.ke on Thursday, June 27.

    Sadly, it was Ochieng' who also works at the same company who rushed from his work station to rescue his wife from the machine but her hand was halfway cut.

    She was rushed to the hospital and had her affected hand amputated.

    Four months later, she is yet to be compensated even though the company had promised to do so.

    Together with her husband, the couple has reported the matter to labour offices but nothing seems to be forthcoming, with threats to sack Ochieng' should he leak the information to the public.

    Despite TUKO.co.ke's Lynn Ngugi visiting the Juja-based company for the other side of the story, the management and Wambui's immediate boss, Joshua, remained cagey and locked out reporters.

    Later on that day, Ochieng' was ordered to hand over the company's property and leave immediately.

    Wambui is now calling for justice for the irreversible damage she suffered while working for the company.
