    08 Aug 2024, 00:00

    • The man identified as Magbee254 Msanii stepped out holding a placard accusing the comedian of giving him a false promise
    • The funnyman told Magbee254 that though he promised to support him, it was not compulsory, he urged him to seek employment from the government

    Kenyan comedian Eric Omondi has responded to a young man who hit the streets with a placard accusing the comedian of not fulfilling his promise to him.

    The upcoming artist identified as Magbee254 Msanii stated on his placard that Eric promised to help release songs but he didn't keep his words.

    Magbee254 also appealed for very support from other celebrities including Kamene Goro, Mwalimu Racheal and Maina Kageni.

    Eric upon noticing the photo of the man while holding a placard, took to Instagram to respond to him.

    The funnyman said that the support he promised to offer Magbee254 was not on compulsion from anyone.

    He told the young man that his studio is currently closed due to a court case and he couldn't help him as he had promised.

    Eric urged Magbee254 to seek employment from the government.

    "Was it by force? Even our studio was closed due to a court case. Wewe Ambia Serikali ikupatie kazi (You tell the government to give you a job)," Eric responded.

    Speaking during an interview with Plug TV, the funnyman said marriage is a scam.

    I'm not marrying anytime soon. I'll get married at 74. Marriage is a scam," the comedian said.
