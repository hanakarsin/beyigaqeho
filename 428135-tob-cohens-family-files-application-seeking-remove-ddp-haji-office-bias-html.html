    Family of slain Dutch tycoon Tob Cohen has moved to the High Court formally seeking the sacking of DPP Noordin Haji whom they accuse of bias in their kin's case.

    In the application filed on Wednesday, September 29, Cohen’s sister, Gabrielle Van Straten, accused Haji of being an impediment in their pursuit of justice two years since the businessman's murder.

    Through their lawyer Danstan Omari, the family faulted the ODPP for its evident lukewarm approach in the case.

    The family expressed its dissatisfaction with Haji after he allegedly failed to instigate charges against Sankale ole Kantai, an appellate judge said to have been engaging Sarah Wairimu, Cohen's widow, in an extra-marital affair.

    The family also held that Sankale worked in cahoots with Cohen's murderers.

    "That it is increasingly becoming clearer to me and the entire world that the office of the Director of Public Prosecutions has some sacred cows and thus the resistance to prosecute the petitioner herein who is not only linked to the murder of my brother but is also painted by the available evidence to have had ex-marital affairs with my sister-in-law (Sarah Wairimu Kamotho) during the lifetime of my late brother," Cohen's sister, Van Straten, said.

    Van Straten added that as a family, they did not figure why the DPP could not charge Sankale alongside those believed to be his accomplices.

    The family further stated that it suspected the ODPP wrote to the Directorate of Criminal Investigation (DCI) stopping his arrest, the family now saying it was a move to absolve the embattled judge.

    Initially, according to reports, a law firm had contacted the DPP seeking to know about the fate of Sankale.

    In his response, the DPP asked the firm to be patient on their enquiry before later contacting the DCI asking the agency to halt any imminent probe on the judge.

    "Is it by accident that the DPP writes two letters one informing Ole Sankale that investigations are going on within a few minutes he writes to the DCI that that file has been closed?" posed the family's counsel."We strongly believe as a family that the DPP is deeply involved in covering up the investigations that will lead to the arrest of justice Kantai to be charged,” added Omari.

    The family's application pertaining to Haji's removal from office now awaits further action by the court.

    Cohen's sister in ongoing case

    In the meantime, Van Straten seeks to be enjoined in the ongoing murder case.

    The woman through her lawyer argued it was unjust and unfair for the proceedings to continue in her absence.

    In a separate application filed in court, Van Straten said unless the application was heard and the orders granted, she would be heavily prejudiced.

    This was after Justice Sankale ole Kantai, a suspect, obtained orders barring his arrest and prosecution.

    “It is obvious that Gabrielle is concerned about the controversy in the instant court proceedings and that unless this court entertains the instant application and gives the appropriate directions, the victim’s voices will be unlawfully muzzled,” Omari said.

    Cohen was found dead in September 2019 after weeks of going missing.

    His body was extracted from a septic tank in his Kitusuru home. His wife Sarah Wairimu Cohen has since been the prime suspect.
