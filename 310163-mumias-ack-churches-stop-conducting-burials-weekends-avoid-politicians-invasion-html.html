    Mumias ACK churches stop conducting burials during weekends to avoid politicians' invasion

    - Mumias ACK churches have banned funerals ceremonies during the weekends - This is in a bid to avoid politicians' interference in the events - It was also found that weekend funerals often clash with other church functions

    03 Feb 2024, 00:00

    - Mumias ACK churches have banned funerals ceremonies during the weekends

    - This is in a bid to avoid politicians' interference in the events

    - It was also found that weekend funerals often clash with other church functions

    Mumias Anglican Church of Kenya ACK bishops have taken drastic action in a bid to put to an end to politicians’ “invasion” of church events.

    TUKO.co.ke has learnt that the the ACK Mumias Diocese has decided not to be holding burials on weekends because such occasions have a tendency of attracting politicians.

    Mumias ACK Bishop Joseph Wandera made the announcement as he explained that the decision had been arrived at after thorough consultations.

    “We recognise and appreciate our political leaders but we have been getting interference from politicians and funerals have been losing religious value as they are turned into political rallies. We shall continue recognising them but we shall not allow them take over our burial services,” he said as quoted by Nation.

    The ban was deemed necessary due to increased concern by the clergymen over the interference of what are supposed to be purely church functions .

    Because of the ban, the diocese will only conduct funeral rites for its members during weekdays.

    Politicking is not the only concern that was raised by the Bishop as it was also noted the funerals during the weekends clashed with other church functions such as fundraisers, weddings and fellowships.

    “The synod meeting in Nambale, Busia county passed a ban that is necessary for a number of reasons, particularly the bloated activities taking place during weekends. Priests and pastors need time to attend to other functions like weddings, fellowships and even to prepare Sunday summons and rest,” he added.

    The move comes at a time when the country is experiencing a strange political transition as the ruling Jubilee party is somewhat split into two warring groups.

    As a result of this, 2022 politics has been in many politicians' mouths and funeral settings have provided a platform for some leaders to spread their agendas, much to the distaste of Wandera.

    On June 8, the Bishop led by example after he walked out of the burial of Jesca Wamatsiin Mung’ang’a because the programme was dominated by politicians including ANC party leader Musalia Mudavadi, former Sports CS Rashid Echesa, Kakamega Senator Cleophas Malala and Lugari MP Ayub Savula

    Following the new church directive, people who have lost their loved ones in Kakamega will be forced to readjust their burial schedules which will include picking the body from the morgue another day other than on Fridays.
