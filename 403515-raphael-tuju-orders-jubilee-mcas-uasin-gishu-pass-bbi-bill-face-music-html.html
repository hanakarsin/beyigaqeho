    Raphael Tuju orders Jubilee MCAs in Uasin Gishu to pass BBI bill or face the music

    - Secretary-General Raphael Tuju hosted 17 Jubilee Party nominated MCAs from Uasin Gishu - He directed them to endorse the BBI bill or risk losing their seats - Uasin Gishi is Deputy President William Ruto's home county and it is highly anticipated the assembly could shoot down the bill

    27 Mar 2024, 00:00

    - Secretary-General Raphael Tuju hosted 17 Jubilee Party nominated MCAs from Uasin Gishu

    - He directed them to endorse the BBI bill or risk losing their seats

    - Uasin Gishi is Deputy President William Ruto's home county and it is highly anticipated the assembly could shoot down the bill

    Jubilee Party secretary-general Raphael Tuju has directed all Jubilee Party nominated MCAs in Uasin Gishu county to vote for BBI bill or risk losing their seats.

    The SG summoned all the 17 nominated ward reps to Nairobi on Friday, February 19, where he issued the firm instruction ahead of the crucial vote next week.

    One of the MCAs who spoke to Daily Nation said Tuju ordered them to support the proposed constitutional amendments as undertaken by the party leadership of risk being subjected to disciplinary action.

    "He (Tuju) told us that we must follow the party position on the BBI Bill, failure of which, we will face disciplinary action,‚Äù said Edwin Misoi, a nominated MCA.

    Public participation

    The ward rep, however, said the country assembly's decision to vote for or against the bill will depend on the views of the people of Uasin Gishu.

    Misoi noted the assembly Legal Committee was conducting public participation and the report it will table to the House will inform the decision they will take.

    Uasin Gishi county is Deputy President William Ruto's backyard and it is highly anticipated the assembly could shoot down the bill.

    Ruto is the only senior politician who has refused to back the BBI bill.

    Riot act

    This was not the first time Tuju summoned and issued firm instructions to Jubilee Party nominated MCAs ahead of the BBI vote.

    On February 10, the SG held a meeting with close to 30 MCAs from Baringo county and urged them to work with their KANU counterparts to pass the handshake bill.

    Tuju also wrote a letter to all Jubilee Party majority leaders in the county assemblies to ensure they whip members to support the legislation.

    In the letter, the spokesperson of the ruling outfit said it would be inexplicable for an MCA elected on Jubilee Party ticket to defy the decision taken by the leadership of the party for the benefit of the country.

    "The party will invoke article 13.3 part (f) and (g) of the party constitution should MCAs fail to adhere to the requirement," read the letter in part. The above article states: "Disciplinary action shall be instituted against a member in cases of (f). Acting or advancing positions contrary to the party position. (g). Failure, neglect or refusal to carry out directives or instructions of the party." "It is inexplicable that any reasonable MCA would find fault with these new advantages that would result from the passage of BBI," added the letter dated February 8.
