    Kirinyaga: 8 Killed While Digging Water Trench, Rescue Operation Underway

    Eight people have been buried alive while digging a water trench in Kirinyanga county.

    The incident happened on Monday, August 30, and rescue efforts by the county rescue team are still underway, pooling efforts to save the lives of the victims.

    The eight who are said to be well workers, were undertaking a water trench project in the Kiandieri area of Kirinyaga central when tragedy struck.

    So far, four people have been rescued from the collapsed section. Unfortunately, one worker has passed on.

    Kirinyaga central deputy commissioner Daniel Ndege said that everything was being done to rescue everyone.

    “The rescue mission is ongoing and we shall give an update with time,” said Ndege.

    He said the county emergency medical team responded swiftly at the scene, with those rescued from the tragedy being rushed to the Kerugoya Level 4 hospital.

    "We thank the media from Kerugoya, who have also acted swiftly to help rescues these lives," he said.

    Kilimani crane

    In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke reported that police officers in Nairobi were investigating circumstances under which at least nine construction workers died at a site.

    Preliminary investigations showed the nine died after a crane they were dismantling crumbled at a construction site in the Kilimani area.

    The incident, which occurred on the afternoon of Thursday, August 26, also left one construction worker with serious injuries.

    The injured was rushed to Nairobi Women's Hospital for emergency treatment.

    Kilimani OCPD Muturi Mbogo confirmed eight people died on the spot during the incident, which happened at the site for the proposed Qwetu residences. Police said among the dead were six Kenyans and two Chinese nationals.

    “We have at least eight bodies at the site after the crane collapsed as it was being dismantled. Investigators are working to know how and why it happened,” he said.

    The proposed 14-floored residential apartment, which is located opposite the Department of Defence headquarters, will have a capacity to accommodate 700 residents once it is complete.
