    Everywhere you look, there is an ongoing project, whether it’s in manufacturing, technology, construction, or teaching. Every project requires a manager for it to succeed, someone who has an eye for details in each step from the beginning to the end. But successful ventures don’t just happen. They are a masterpiece of management, planning and communication; a sequence of events that are carefully choreographed with smooth and steady progress while countering any possible hitch. They are different project management courses in Kenya that one can enrol in the accredited institution to become the best manager.

    In Kenya, there has been a growing demand for project management skills in the workforce. As a result of this, Kenyan universities and colleges are providing project management courses that meet the industry’s need. If you wish to pursue a career in project management, or even top it up to your skills, you now have the opportunity of attaining quality project management courses from Kenyan institutions.

    These institutions offer accredited courses that help students get to their goals, whether they are new to the job market, professionals seeking to strengthen their resume by staying up-to-date with new techniques, or professionals who are considering a career change. We have pinned down a list of the best colleges and universities offering project management courses in Kenya, including the requirements.

    1. Masters of Science in Project Management/ Masters of Arts in Project Planning and Management

    The objective of the program is to equip learners with knowledge and experience in organising, planning, and managing projects in a way that stimulates productivity and innovativeness. The course targets students aspiring to be project management professionals, project managers, project analysts, consultants, and company executives.

    READ ALSO: Marketable technical courses in Kenya

    Key learning

    Upon finishing the course, the student will be in a position to:

    • Research on project management issues.
    • Handle and manage development projects.
    • Describe managerial, organisational, human behaviour, and financial problems that are relevant to project management.
    • Understand the theories and principles that guide project management.

    Duration

    • Two years: Three semesters for course work and one for project thesis.

    Entry requirements

    • A holder of a Bachelor’s degree with at least Upper Second Class Honors in any discipline from a recognised institution.
    • A holder of a Bachelor’s degree with Lower Second Class Honors in any discipline from a known institution and has proven research ability or experience of 3 years in a relevant field.
    • A holder of a Bachelor’s degree with a Pass in any discipline from a recognised institution and has proven research ability or experience of 5 years in a relevant field.

    College

    The postgraduate program is offered in the following institutions:

    • University of Nairobi
    • Jomo Kenyatta University of Science and Technology which offers both JKUAT eLearning and on-campus mode.

    2. Bachelors in Project Management

    The course teaches learners to practice good and quality project management. Learners will be able to identify and plan for projects, create project flow charts, schedule project resources, provide evaluation reports, selection of the right staff, and proper team management. The learning objective will enable the students to use the tools and apply the principles they have learnt, in the workforce.

    Key learning

    • Through the project management course, you will learn the importance of time management, goals setting and prioritisation of tasks.
    • The training will enable you to determine how to estimate resources required to successfully undertake a project including human resources, budget allocation and time.
    • You will learn how to document every step of a project. It is useful for future reference.
    • It will train you on how to work with information systems since, in today’s age, projects are not done manually; instead, several programs and applications help in tracking the milestones of projects.

    Duration

    • The course takes a minimum of four years.

    Entry requirements

    • A minimum grade of C+ in KCSE with a minimum of C Plain in English/Kiswahili and a C Plain in Mathematics.
    • A diploma or professional certificate holder in a relevant discipline from a recognised institution.

    3. Diploma in Project Management

    It aims at equipping students with skills that will make them competent in acquiring project management jobs in Kenya. Students who possess the knowledge, skills and experience necessary to manage projects successfully, will increasingly be in demand in this professional field that is growing at a fast pace.

    Learning outcomes

    Project management Kenya courses aim to:

    • Enable the learner to conceive, develop and implement projects in both the private and public sector.
    • Perform various simple project management tasks.

    Duration

    The course duration for a diploma in Project Management is 1 - 2 years as it varies from one institution to another.

    Entry requirements

    • A minimum grade of C- or C Plain in KCSE (varies from one institution to another).
    • A certificate/ diploma/ advanced diploma/ degree holder in any field.
