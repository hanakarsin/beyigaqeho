    President Uhuru Kenyatta, his deputy William Ruto and Opposition leader Raila Odinga are also the focus of the newspapers, especially after Saturday, January 8, remarks when the president said, "maybe it was time for another tribe to lead".

    1. Daily Nation

    A section of political leaders from the Mount Kenya region is piling pressure on President Uhuru Kenyatta, as the region's kingpin, to name his successor in a bid to tame the fast-growing popularity of his deputy William Ruto in the region.

    Some of the political heavyweights from the mountain have expressed fear that their jobs will be on the line if the president will not move with speed and calm the disquiet.

    Among those who have urged the president to name an heir is former Gatanga MP Peter Kenneth who is Uhuru's perceived heir apparent.

    "If danger is looming you don't want to take a lot of time. We can see danger looming in our country. There is no time to waste. People need to sort the problem now going forward Kenya, deserve the best. What we are saying in Mount Kenya is that we want to be on the table and we decide who goes to that table," said Kenneth.

    The naming of a successor may, however, be complicated, especially after Uhuru's remarks that maybe it is time for another tribe to rule Kenya. The elephant in the room is if the Mount Kenya region will be receptive to a move by the president to name a successor who is not from their fold.

    Uhuru's recent remarks suggested he was not going to back Ruto.

    2. People Daily

    The daily reports about a frosty relationship between the Office of the Chief Justice, Attorney General and the Director of Public Prosecution (DPP).

    The two, AG Kihara Kariuki and DPP Noordin Haji, were absent during the exit ceremony for David Maraga who retired as the Chief Justice on midnight of Monday, January 11.

    Kihara and Kariuki were invited to the ceremony and were supposed to sit near LSK president Nelson Havi.

    Judiciary officials had to remove their names from designated sitting positions.

    Their absence at the event could be a pointer to a bumpy ride awaiting acting Chief Justice Philomena Mwilu.

    3. The Standard

    The daily reports that David Maraga's successor in the office of Chief Justice will be tasked with a daunting task of mending the strained relationship between the Judiciary and the Executive.

    Maraga's tenure at the helm of the Judiciary was punctured with constant fire to and from the Executive, with Maraga accusing Executive of budget cuts.

    On its part, the Executive blamed Maraga for demanding too much and failing to tame corruption.

    Among those who are deemed to be possible successors of Maraga are DCJ Philomena Mwilu (now acting CJ), Supreme court Judge Njoki Ndungu, Justice Smokin Wanjala, former AG Githu Muigai and Justice Martha Koome.

    Others are Court of Appeal Judge William Ouko, Justice Isaac Lenaola and Lawyer Abdikadir Mohammed.

    4. Taifa Leo

    The Swahili daily has described the recent exchange of words between Uhuru and his deputy Ruto as a recipe for disaster in the country.

    On Saturday, Uhuru said, maybe it was time for another tribe, apart from Kikuyu and Kalenjins to rule Kenya, remarks which were not taken lightly by his DP.

    Ruto responded stating that he supported and voted for Uhuru, not because of his tribe but because he believed in his leadership.

    Observers believe the exchange between the duo is poisonous for the country especially at this time when the country is nearing the 2022 General Election. They argue that the duo needs to display neutrality in their speeches even though they do not agree on most matters.
