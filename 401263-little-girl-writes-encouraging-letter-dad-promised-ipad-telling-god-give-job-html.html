    Children have an amazing–if not selective–memories. For instance, a child might not be able to remember to put on actual shoes before getting in the car to run errands with their mom.

    But if a promise is made one day that they will get ice cream after school, you can bet it will be the first thing out of their mouths when they finish classes on that day. Fascinating.

    That was what happened to one Twitter user identified as Taiwo Longe who had promised to get his daughter an iPad on her sixth birthday which happened to be on Wednesday, January 28.

    Longe's daughter did not forget that promise and as she left for school, she wrote a letter, which he shared on his Twitter handle, to her father reminding him of the promise he had made a long time ago.

    In the heartwarming missive, the little girl said she understood that her father did not have enough money to buy her the gift he had promised.

    She went on and encouraged her father saying God would bless him with a better job and more money.

    "Do not kill my chicken, go and sell it, take the money and use it to buy me the iPad," she wrote.

    She even specified the colour of the iPad that she wanted and the shop where her father should buy it from.

    While a number of Longe's followers were proud of his six-year-old daughter's grasp of the English language, others thought there was no way she could have written the letter.
