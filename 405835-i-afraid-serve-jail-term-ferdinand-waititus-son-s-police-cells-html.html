    I am not afraid to serve jail term, Ferdinand Waititu's son says he's not new to police cells

    Former Kiambu Governor Ferdinand Waititu's last born son was on Monday, March 15 charged in court for drink-driving.

    Brian Ndungu Waititu who is a law student at Nairobi's Strathmore University was charged with driving under the influence of a drink contrary to Section 44(1) of the Traffic Act.

    He was accused of driving on a public road while under the influence of alcohol.

    The accused who was not represented by an advocate was to be freed on a fine of KSh 30,000 or a serve 3-month term in jail upon pleading guilty.

    He was said to have been drunk "to such an extent of being incapable of having proper control of the motor vehicle, contrary to section 44(1) of the Traffic Act. Ndung'u was said to have had 1.068 mg/l of alcohol instead of 0.350mg/l in his body.

    The accused who was not represented by an advocate was to be freed on a fine of KSh30,000 or a serve 3-month term in jail upon pleading guilty.

    Speaking to TUKO.co.ke, Ndung'u said he was unable to raise the bail saying he is not afraid to serve the 3 months in jail.

    "I have been arrested more than 15 times so going to jail for me is not a big deal," he said.

    He added that his father, ex-governor Ferdinand Waititu, was not aware of his latest charges.

    Ndung'u said whenever he gets arrested he never calls his father for help.

    "My old man cannot be bothered by my being arrested, I have been arrested more than five times for violating curfew rules," he said.

    The 25-year-old revealed that he lives in separate servant quarters in his parents home as he admitted being drunk at the time of his arrest.
