    Late Shanty's father files case with IPOA, claims police officers were compromised

    - Shanty's dad Mr Gikonyo recently filed a report at the IPOA in a bid to bring his son's killers to book - Unconfirmed reports indicate that some police officers who were handling the case had been compromised

    08 Jun 2024, 00:00

    - Shanty's dad Mr Gikonyo recently filed a report at the IPOA in a bid to bring his son's killers to book

    - Unconfirmed reports indicate that some police officers who were handling the case had been compromised

    - The grieving dad was forced to head to IPOA after his son's case stalled at Kabete Police Station

    Losing a son or daughter is one pain that no one has ever imagined to deal with, owing to its depth.

    It is even more painful if the deaths were caused by people you know and with enough evidence out to pin them, but in vain.

    The aforementioned, is exactly what one Mr Geoffrey Gikonyo, the father of late Shanty has been going through in the past few months.

    For those in the dark, Shanty is the 17-year-old form three student who died after being beaten at the Nai Fest which was held on Saturday, November 7, at the Ngong Race Course.

    TUKO.co.ke had earlier reported how Shanty's dad and other notable Kenyans came out to seek justice for the dead teen.

    News reaching our news desk intimate that Shanty's dad Mr Gikonyo is still following up his son's case months after his demise and recently filed a report at the IPOA in a bid to bring his son's killers to book.

    According to evidence accessed by TUKO.co.ke, Gikonyo decided to head to IPOA after learning some police officers from Kabete Police Station who were handling the case had been compromised.

    It is not yet clear if they were bribed or jeaopardised in other ways but the case stalled, forcing the old man to seek help elsewhere.

    Gikonyo, in her chats with our source pleaded with Kenyans who witnessed what his son went through to come out in large umbers and help him by also filing reports at IPOA over the same.

    ''Afternoon, is it possible for the same youths to record a statement with IPOA? I am still pushing on. Will appreciate if you organise them and give me a date and venue. IPOA can come along to record statements anywhere. I want to pass that information to senior investigators at IPOA. Please help me get justice for Shanty.'' Gikonyo told our source.

    The latest development comes about two months after TUKO.co.ke first reported about Shanty's death.

    Shanty had attended Nai Fest where he was manhandled before being found days later.

    Video footage shared online showed how some well built men, alleged to be bouncers beat up the young boy, whose body was found at the Nairobi City Mortuary, about five days later.

    Different sources indicated that Shanty was beaten seriously and rushed to hospital, but how he ended up at the City Mortuary remained a mystery.

    The victim's dad, who spoke to journalists for the first time at the Kabete Police Station where Shanty’s case was being handled said he had permitted his son to spend some time with his friends but did not know he will never see him again.

    Shanty's dad pleaded with Police to look into the matter and arrest people who killed his son and who were caught on camera beating him.

    Several public figures such as The Black Cinderella, Nonini, Frasha and Mongolo jumped in as they tried to help Shanty's dad get justice for his son.

    Even with the latest development, the organiser of the event socialite Bridget Achieng still denies Shanty died at her event.
