    A boy from Isiolo county, who scored 378 marks in the 2020 Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCPE) examination, is appealing for financial help to join Form One.

    Khalid Hassan, who sat for his KCPE examination at Isiolo Barracks Primary School, was been selected to join Meru School.

    No school fees for Khalid Hassan

    According to Hassan, he tried applying for different scholarships among them Equity Bank's Qings to Fly, KCB, and Elimu without success.

    " I have tried applying for different scholarships and have also gone for their interviews without success," Hassan stated.

    The young learner said his family encouraged him to work hard and score marks that will propel him to land a scholarship, but this has not happened despite his good marks.

    "I really worked hard as I was told, knowing tmy background very well, the scholarship was the only thing that could have helped me out. I performed well, scored 378 and managed to go to several interviews for those scholarships but to no success," Hassan continued.

    Interview with Equity Wings to Fly

    The brainy boy divulged that when he was called for an interview by Equity Wings to Fly, he even talked with the manager, who assured him that they will take him.

    However, when the beneficiary's names were out, his name was missing.

    He stated that he is supposed to join on August 2, but his mother being a single parent, has not even been able to raise even the money for shopping leave alone school fees.

    On her part, the boy's mother said her attempts to reach local leaders to help his son have been futile.

    "I have tried all I can, but I have not succeeded. I have walked and talked with people whom I thought can help me. I even recorded myself crying begging for help then sent it to one of the politicians, but he never responded," she said.

    She said it reached a point where she told his son that she will not join school since she cannot afford the school fees and was the sole breadwinner in the family.

    "I ran a business which is giving me KSh 300 per day, and that goes to food alone; I can not afford his school fees. That is why I am appealing to well-wishers to come to our aid and help my son join because he really performed well, and he deserves it," she added.

    Government offers scholarships to 62 in Mandera

    Early this week, the national government offered full scholarships to 62 needy students worth KSh 3 million from vulnerable families in Mandera county through the Constituency Development Fund kitty.

    In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke reported that John Felix Osuro, who sat for his KCPE exam in March 2021, was on the verge of missing admission to secondary school due to a lack of school fees.

    Felix scored 373 marks in the KCPE exams and was also the top pupil at Pundo Primary School.

    He said he hoped to join Maranda High School in Siaya county and later enrol in an engineering course at the university.
