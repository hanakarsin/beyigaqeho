    An elderly man and his wife have been thrown out of their home after their kin failed to honour a debt owed to two shylocks.

    Identified as Francis William Balyokwayibwe, the 68-year-old, who is a retired banker, had been used as a guarantor in a loan secured by his cousin, a Barbra, from the shylock (identified as Doreen).

    William was a manager at Stanbic Bank in Uganda from 1977 to 2005 when he retired.

    Barbra reportedly used the retired banker's title to secure a KSh 2.2 million loan from the said money lender in July 2018.

    She said she was expecting a lot of money and shared a plan with William who agreed to the loan agreement at the time, and offered his land title for the same.

    According to reports, the loan taken from Doreen was to be repaid monthly at a constant 20% interest.

    The loan was to be used by Barbra's family in a cereal export business.

    However, the family defaulted on the loan for almost a year which accumulated to KSh 6.5 million.

    After a series of discussions, the loaner and the loanee managed to negotiate a reduction which brought the loan value and interest to KSh 5.6 million from the initial KSh 6.5 million.

    Barbra secures a second loan to service the first one

    Under pressure to clear the outstanding KSh 5.6 million loan, Barbra engaged a different money lender (identified as Francis Mutabaazi) from whom she secured KSh 6.8 million, which she intended to use in clearing the first loan and channelling the balance to their struggling cereal business.

    However, she failed to service both loans, prompting the lenders to seek the intervention of the courts.

    The loans have now taken a toll on William and his wife after guaranteeing their cousin, Barbra.

    The couple said they could no longer cannot access their home after court bailiffs kicked them out, with their belongings over non-payment of the loans, which have now accumulated to KSh 12.4 million.

    The family was evicted on the basis of an order issued on May 18, 2021, from Uganda's Nabweru Chief Magistrates Court to Shrewd Solutions Limited, indicating that William, a tenant in Mutabaazi’s house had failed to pay rent for more than a year.

    She said the initial loan was also shared between the two families, but was not enough to clear the challenges that the family had at the time, forcing them to pursue additional loans to keep them afloat.

    Mutabaazi, the said moneylender said that the matter will be handled by the courts of law.
