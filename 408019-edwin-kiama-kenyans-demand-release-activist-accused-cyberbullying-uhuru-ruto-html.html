    Kenyans have come out to demand unconditional release of activist Edwin Kiama who is in police custody on grounds of cyberbullying President Uhuru Kenyatta and his deputy William Ruto.

    Kiama was arrested on Tuesday, April 6, and accused of making posters against the two top leaders, condemning them for the ballooning public debt.

    Taking to social media from Tuesday night to the better part of Wednesday, April 7, netizens demanded Kiama be freed with many noting his freedom of speech was being infringed.

    "The police are accusing him of making the below public notice asking the world to stop lending money to Kenya. The money Uhuruto borrows is stolen," activist Boniface Mwangi tweeted.

    A Twitter user identified as Bass said no one should be subjected to intimidation because of fighting for a noble cause and thus called the state to free the activist.

    Hussein Mohammed said:

    "Cowards don't live longer. They suffer longer! It's everyone's duty to speak up and fight injustice and tyranny." #ReleaseKiama

    Makeveli theDon said:

    "But where is the lie with the post. I support it completely and they should release him."

    On Wednesday, the activist was arraigned at the Milimani Law Courts where he maintained he was unapologetic for making the controversial posters.

    In the viral graphics, Kiama warned the International Monetary Fund (IMF) and other international lenders to refrain from loaning Kenya any more money.

    So far, over 210,000 Kenyans have signed a petition asking the IMF to cancel the KSh 255 billion it gave the Jubilee government on Friday, April 2.

    Over the past few days, hashtag #IMFStopLoaningKenya has been trending on Twitter with many Kenyans expressing their frustrations over the massive loans being borrowed by the country.

    Many said the lenders should first audit the money it had given Kenyans in the past.
