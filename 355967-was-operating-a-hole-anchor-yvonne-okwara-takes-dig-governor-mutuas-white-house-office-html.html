    Was he operating from a hole?: Anchor Yvonne Okwara takes dig at Governor Mutua's White House office

    Machakos governor Alfred Mutua's newly built expansive and magnificent office has continued to evoke emotions from Kenyans.

    News anchor Yvonne Okwara has become the latest Kenyan to tore into governor's vast mansion built at cost of KSh 350 million amid numerous challenges facing Machakos residents.

    Speaking on Thursday, May 7, night, Yvonne questioned whether the luxurious building constructed in just nine months was the most urgent thing Machakos residents needed at this point in time.

    "Was this the most urgent need for this county? For the last seven years or so where has the governor been operating from? On a tree? A hole in the ground? The streets maybe? Was this the one trying the people of Machakos needed at this point in time? questioned Yvonne.

    The journalist also wondered whether the office was the governor's only unfulfilled dream project for the electorates who have for long been grappling with perennial challenges of water shortage.

    "Of all the things the people of Machakos desperately need; water, access to food, quality and affordable healthcare, roads, agricultural and so on, have these expectations met? Even surpassed? So much so that there nothing left for the governor to do with so much spare change of a few hundred millions sitting in the bank that he decided to build an office? the news anchor posed.

    In his speech during the launch of the office in Masaku, Mutua said he chose to put up a structure that would last to serve many generations to come including the unborn.

    "We must build structures, we must build physical entities that will ensure devolution is set, I have built an office for the public, other counties have built governor's residences. I have decided to use the money to build an office for the public, not a home for one person. This is not about me, it is about perpetuation, it is about devolution, it is about our children who are not born today and I hope others will get to understand that as leaders we must do things that have a vision," said Mutua
