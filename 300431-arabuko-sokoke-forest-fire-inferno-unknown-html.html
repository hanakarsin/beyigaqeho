    Arabuko-Sokoke forest on fire, cause of inferno unknown

    -Firefighters, KFS and Red Cross officials are in the area to contain the fire - A large section of the forest has already been burned down - Mt Kenya Forest has also suffered a similar fate on the recent past

    26 Apr 2024, 00:00

    -Firefighters, KFS and Red Cross officials are in the area to contain the fire

    - A large section of the forest has already been burned down

    - Mt Kenya Forest has also suffered a similar fate on the recent past

    A huge fire was on Monday, March 11, reported at the Arabuko Sokoke forest in Malindi Sub-county.

    According to senior warden in charge of Kenya Forest Service (KFS) in the forest, Charles Wamola, the cause of the fire has not yet been identified.

    TUKO.co.ke understands the inferno consumed a large area and has not been contained yet.

    Speaking via phone, Wamola said the KFS together with Red Cross officers and firefighters were in the area to contain the blaze from spreading to more areas of the forest.

    “Our officers together with Red Cross crew and firefighter have responded to the fire in effort to contain it before it causes more damage,” he said.

    READ ALSO: Plane forced to turn back after mother forgets her newborn at airport

    He also said investigations had been launched while calling upon locals to be more careful and avoid the forest area which borders some small villages.

    Arabuko-Sokoke Forest is located south of Gede in Kilifi county and has been used in study of various animal and plants species for the last 100 years in Kenya.

    The inferno broke out a few weeks after another one erupted at the Mount Kenya National Park consuming over 120,000 hectares of vegetation.
