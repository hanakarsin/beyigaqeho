    What are safe days to avoid pregnancy?

    Although a woman can get pregnant at any time of the month when she has sex without contraception use, there are certain days in the menstrual cycle when she is least likely to get pregnant after having unprotected vaginal sex with her partner. These days are what is referred to as the safe days to have sex.

    Safe days in a 28-day cycle

    The use of safe days is a natural family planning method that is sometimes usually referred to as the calendar method. Before using the calendar method as a form of family planning, you need to keep track of your periods for at least six cycles.

    If all your periods last less than 27 days, this method is not suitable for you. So, how do you calculate the safe days to have unprotected sex with your partner?

    • Find the shortest cycle in your past six-cycle record.
    • Subtract 18 days from the total number of days in the shortest cycle.
    • Count that number from the first day of your current cycle and mark it with a red pen.
    • If your shortest cycle happens to be 28 days, if you subtract 18 from 28, you'll have ten days left with you.

    Safe days calculator

    The safe days' calculator makes your work even simpler as it helps you know your safe days easily. There is only one thing required of you when using the calculator -- remember the first day of your menstrual cycle, the longest and shortest days on your cycle in a period of 6 months.

    The calculator then lets you key in the dates as requested, then automatically calculates for you your safe days for not getting pregnant.

    The downside of safe days

    The major flaw with this method is that it requires discipline, which could be hard to practice with partners too close or living together. Again this method is not recommended for those whose cycle is either irregular or exceeds 28 days.

    For those having sexual relations solely for pleasure and not procreation, this method is not for you, and you should consider other less risky alternatives.

    Signs of ovulation

    When you're working out safe days, you should watch out for the signs of ovulation. Ovulation occurs one day after the fertile window, and you need to abstain from unprotected vaginal sex to prevent pregnancy. Here are some of the signs that you should watch out for to know if you are ovulating:

    • Mild cramping in your lower abdomen goes away after some time.
    • Increased basal body temperature.
    • Increased urge to have sex.
    • A wet and clear discharge from the vagina, which looks like egg white in colour.

    To be certain of your ovulation, you can also acquire ovulation kits, which will help you know the exact ovulation day in your cycle.

    If you are searching for a natural and safe family planning method, then safe days to avoid pregnancy should be on top of your list. But keep in mind, your cycle days can still change each month based on various factors such as stress, diet, or heavy exercise. If you have irregular cycles, speak to your doctor about the best birth control plan for you.
