    Over the years, women have continued to defy societal norms and barriers to achieve a lot in tennis. It is even hard to pick out the greatest female tennis players of all time, considering that most have excelled in the sport. Nonetheless, a few have set outstanding records, and though some have retired, many fans still remember them.

    Having your name listed among the greatest female tennis players of all time is not an easy task. It is so because numerous female athletes have graced the court with impressive skills. However, some tennis players have inspired many in the sport.

    The greatest female tennis players of all time

    Top tennis players dominated their peers, and most of them had games that could translate into success in other eras. Here are the names of the greatest female tennis players of all time.

    15. Martina Hingis

    • Country: Switzerland
    • Date of birth: September 30th, 1980
    • Turned pro: 1994
    • Year of retirement: 2017
    • Grand Slam titles: 5
    • Career titles: 45
    • Prize money winnings: $24.7 million

    With 5 Grand Slam singles championships and 209 weeks as the world's number one, Martina ranks among the best. She became famous as a young star, and that status brought her grand success in her career. She set a series of "youngest-eve" records before ligament injuries.

    Does Martina Hingis have a child? She got married to Harald Leemann in 2018. The couple welcomed their daughter Lia in 2019.

    14. Jennifer Maria Capriati

    • Country: United States of America
    • Date of birth: March 29th, 1976
    • Turned pro: 1990
    • Year of retirement: 2004
    • Grand slam titles: 3
    • Career titles: 14
    • Prize money winnings: $10.2 million
    • Inducted into Tennis Hall of Fame: 2009

    Jennifer Capriati is a member of the International Tennis Hall of Fame. She won three singles Grand Slam tournaments and was the gold medalist at the 1992 Summer Olympics. However, many people think of her as the child star who got too much early in her career.

    13. Margaret Court

    • Country: Australia
    • Year of birth: July 16th, 1942
    • Turned pro: 1960
    • Year of retirement: 1977
    • Career titles: 192 (Singles)
    • Grand Slam titles: 24 (Singles)
    • Prize money winnings: $500,000
    • Inducted into Tennis Hall of Fame: 1979

    Margaret Court retired from the sport in 1977. However, her record in the sport will echo to eternity. She won 24 Grand Slam singles titles, 19 Grand Slam doubles championships, and 21 Grand Slam mixed doubles titles.

    12. Justine Henin

    • Country: Belgium
    • Date of birth: June 1st, 1982
    • Turned pro: 1999
    • Retired: 2011
    • Grand Slam titles: 7 (Singles)
    • Career titles: 50
    • Prize money winnings: $20.9 million

    Justine is famous for her mental and physical tenacity. She is, without a doubt, one of the most athletic women to ever play the game. She did enough to creep onto this list of the greatest female tennis players of all time.

    Henin was ranked number one in the world in 2003 after winning both the French and US Opens. She also earned a gold medal at the Athens Olympics in 2004 to add to her maiden Australian Open victory.

    11. Tracy Ann Austin Holt

    • Country: United States of America
    • Date of birth: December 12th, 1962
    • Turned pro: 1978
    • Year of retirement: 1994
    • Grand Slam titles: 2
    • Career titles: 30 (Singles)
    • Prize money winnings: $2 million

    Tracy Austin was only 16 years old when she won the U.S. Open in 1979. She followed it up with another victory in 1981. She has achieved a lot in her career.

    Early in her career, injuries piled up for her and put a damper on any significant comeback. She was a highly well-rounded player who could thrive on every surface. However, she was unable to stay at the top of the game for long.

    10. Steffi Graf

    • Country: Germany
    • Date of birth: June 14th, 1969
    • Turned pro: 1982
    • Year or retirement: 1999
    • Grand Slam titles: 22
    • Career titles: 107
    • Prize money winnings: $21.9 million
    • Inducted into Tennis Hall of Fame: 2004

    Graf won a ton of success in the 1990s in particular. However, it was in 1988 where she first made a huge splash. She took home the Golden Grand Slam, four major titles, and the Olympic gold in the Seoul Olympics.

    Throughout her career, the German was consistent on all surfaces. Her 377-week record at No. 1 remains unbroken. She is married to Andre Agassi, and they have given birth to two children; Jaden Gil and Jaz Elle Agassi.

    9. Monica Seles

    • Country: SFR Yugoslavia
    • Date of birth: December 2nd, 1973
    • Turned pro: 1989
    • Year of retirement: 2008
    • Grand Slam titles: 9
    • Career titles: 53 (Singles)
    • Prize money winnings: $14.9 million
    • Inducted into Tennis Hall of Fame: 2009

    Read also

    Monica Seles became a star in the early 90s, and by the age of 19, she had won eight Grand Slam titles. She had a style of play that was well ahead of her time. She was named one of the "30 Legends of Women's Tennis: Past, Present and Future" by Time in 2011.

    In 1993, her career was stopped temporarily by a stabbing incident. A deranged fan of rival Steffi Graf ran onto the court during a match and stabbed her in the back.

    8. Lindsay Davenport

    • Country: United States of America
    • Date of Birth: June 8th, 1976
    • Turned pro: 1993
    • Year of retirement: 2010
    • Grand slam titles: 3
    • Career titles: 55 (Singles)
    • Prize money winnings: $22.2 million

    Lindsay had a modern power game, relying on groundstrokes from both wings to pound opponents from the baseline. As a result, she gained massive popularity among tennis fans even though some players overlooked her.

    Davenport now works as a commentator for Tennis Channel. She has also served as the coach of current elite player Madison Keys. The former top-ranked player is still heavily involved in the comings and goings of the sport.

    7. Venus Williams

    • Country: United States of America
    • Date of birth: June 17th, 1980
    • Turned pro: 1994
    • Grand Slam titles: 7 (Singles)
    • Career titles: 49 (Singles)
    • Prize money winnings: $41.7 million

    Like her sister Serena, Venus Williams' name can not miss among the best female tennis players of all time. She is a former world number one in both singles and doubles.

    With a large serve and incredible groundstrokes, Venus Williams has won seven Grand Slam titles. At the beginning of her career, many assumed she would be the next dominant star. However, her skills could not match those of her younger sister's.

    6. Billie Jean King

    • Country: United States of America
    • Date of birth: November 22nd, 1943
    • Turned pro: 1959
    • Year of retirement: 1983
    • Grand Slam titles: 12
    • Career titles: 129 (Singles)
    • Prize money winnings: $1.9 million
    • Inducted into Tennis Hall of Fame: 1987

    During her active years, Billie Jean King was an outstanding player. She made 12 Grand Slam wins; six of them were won at Wimbledon from 1966 to 1975. She was a charging player who had no fear of serving and volleys.

    Billie is well-known for her victory over former Wimbledon men's champion Bobby Riggs in the Battle of Sexes in 1973.

    5. Christine Marie Evert

    • Country: United States of America
    • Date of birth: December 21st, 1954
    • Turned pro: 1972
    • Year of retirement: 1989
    • Career titles: 157 (Singles)
    • Grand Slam titles: 18 (Singles)
    • Prize money winnings: $8,895,195
    • Inducted into Tennis Hall of Fame: 1995

    Chris Evert was the undisputed star of the 1970s. Going by statistics, it is hard to keep her out of the top position. She held the title of world's number one for seven years.

    Evert was involved in a few big rivalries in her career. However, the biggest was against Martina Navratilova. She had a fantastic career, winning 18 Grand Slam titles.

    4. Evonne Goolagong Cawley

    • Country: Australia
    • Date of birth: July 31st, 1951
    • Turned pro: 1970
    • Year of retirement: 1983
    • Grand Slam titles: 7 (Singles)
    • Career titles: 86 (Singles)
    • Prize money winnings: $1.4 million
    • Inducted into Tennis Hall of Fame: 1988

    Evonne Goolagong managed to win seven Grand Slam titles in one of the most challenging times of women's tennis. She was ranked number 1 female tennis player in 1976. She remains the first mother to have won Wimbledon after the First World War.

    Evonne retired from the sport in 1983. However, her remarkable contribution to the sport makes her one of the top female tennis players. She is married to Roger Cawley, and they gave birth to two children; Kelly Inalla and Morgan Cawley.

    3. Maria Yuryevna Sharapova

    • Country: Russia
    • Date of birth: April 19th, 1987
    • Turned pro: 2001
    • Grand Slam titles: 5 (Singles)
    • Career titles: 40 (Singles)
    • Prize money winnings: $38.7 million

    The Russian star became the world No. 1 for the first time on August 22nd, 2005, at 18. She is the first Russian female tennis player to top the singles rankings. It's difficult to forget her career Grand Slam and five championships.

    Maria Sharapova has strong strokes and a precise serve. She was a WTA World No. 1 for a total of 21 weeks.

    2. Martina Navratilova

    • Country: Czech, United States of America
    • Date of birth: October 18th, 1956
    • Turned pro: 1975
    • Year of retirement: 2006
    • Grand Slam titles: 18 (Singles)
    • Career titles: 168 (Singles)
    • Prize money winnings: $21.6 million
    • Inducted into Tennis Hall of Fame: 2000

    Martina Navratilova is yet another best female tennis player you can not fail to list among the top. The retired Czech-American had one of the most successful tennis careers in history. She won 18 Grand Slam titles.

    Regarded as the most dominant in tennis, Navratilova won 428 of 442 singles matches over five consecutive seasons.

    1. Serena Jameka Williams

    • Country: United States of America
    • Date of birth: September 26th, 1981
    • Turned pro: 1995
    • Grand Slam titles: 23 (Singles)
    • Career titles: 72 (Singles)
    • Prize money winnings: $94 million

    Talk about famous female tennis players, and the name Serena Williams will always come up. The American star has been a special one in the sport since 1995. She has won 23 Grand Slam titles, second to Margaret Court, in the sport's history.

    The Women's Tennis Association ranked her singles world number one on eight separate occasions between 2002 and 2017. She is still active in the sport, and at 40 years (as of 2021), she is still winning awards. Her $94 million in career prize money is twice as much as any other female athlete has made.
